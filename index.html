Predictive Uniformity & Matter Formation

Summary: Large-scale uniformity and matter emerge from predictive bias dynamics within the informational field.

Equation (9): Predictive Bias Diffusion Law

The predictive bias diffuses and equalizes across neighboring regions:

\[ \frac{\partial B(x,t)}{\partial t} = \eta \nabla^2 B(x,t) \]

Discrete form: \[ B_{t+1}(x) = B_t(x) + \sum_{\Delta x}\,\eta\,C(x,\Delta x)\,[\,B_t(\Delta x)-B_t(x)\,] \]

Equation (10): Predictive Bias Divergence & Matter Formation

Local structures “freeze” when predictive divergence exceeds a critical threshold:

\[ D(x,t)=\lvert\nabla B(x,t)\rvert^2 \]

\[ \frac{\partial B}{\partial t} = \begin{cases} \eta \nabla^2 B, & D(x,t) < D_c\\ 0, & D(x,t) \geq D_c \end{cases} \]

When \(D \ge D_c\), diffusion halts locally — the frozen bias manifests as matter or structure.

Physical Analogy

Heat-like diffusion of \(B\) → uniformity (Eq. 9). Phase-transition-like locking at \(D_c\) → matter (Eq. 10).
